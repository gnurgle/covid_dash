Questions and Answers

Where does the information come from? What are your sources?

All information comes directly from the Florida Department of Health's databases here: https://open-fdoh.hub.arcgis.com/search?q=covid19
The information for the vaccines come from their daily report here: http://ww11.doh.state.fl.us/comm/_partners/covid19_report_archive/vaccine/vaccine_report_latest.pdf
The map on the front page is an embedded version of the one from the FL DOH COVID Dashboard here: https://experience.arcgis.com/experience/96dd742462124fa0b38ddedb9b25e429

How are the numbers in the breakdowns calculated?

The numbers are reached by subtracting the previous day with the latest number. If the range is for 7 days then that number is used instead. The information is kept as a total and is overwritten each day on the FL DOH side, so this dashboard keeps a local copy along with a datastamp.

Why are the numbers in the breakdown different from other results?

By using the subtraction method, a fairly accurate number can be achieved. This number will never be 100% accurate as data is updated after the day it is posted. Thus only the difference between what was reported at two different dates can be presented since the original data is overwritten by the DOH on update.

Why are there negative numbers on the breakdown?

The negative numbers are a prime example of when some information has been updated. Some commonplaces are data related to non-residents and data that is listed as unknown. When more information is recieved, these are updated so the total number will change from one day to another, possibly going down. This is a limitation of the subtraction method used and the was that the FL DOH presents the information.

How is the Age Group calculated in the breakdown?

Instead of using the subtraction method, this counts the number of positive patients directly since that information is available in a much more detailed format. These numbers should represent 100% accuracy with what the FL DOH has on record as they are simply an aggregate query of the patient database.

How is the percent calculated in the vaccines?

The population of the county divided by the total amount of vaccines in the county times 100 gives the percentage. The exceptions are Unknown and Out of State which will always show 0.

Why do the numbers on this dashboards differ from other dashboards?

Other dashboards might add or subtract certain things like antibody counts or non-residents. The purpose of this dashboard is to be 100% transparent in how any calculations are done and to do as little changing of the data as possible. The only agenda is providing an open-sourced, non-biased dashboard that presents as much of the information as possible.

Why is (insert information) not included?

Some of the information may be out of date or slow to update (antibody testing, Long-term care facilities), have good summaries readily available (school report), something that I don't have enough data for yet (30-day county breakdown), or was something simply not included (more thorough look at testing).

Where can I find the source code?

It should be avaliable here: https://github.com/gnurgle/covid_dash
There are instruction on how to set it up and run it on your local system.
It's important that not only the information, but the tools to analyze it is available to everyone. No one should be profiting off of simply displaying public information, let alone information related to a world health pandemic.
